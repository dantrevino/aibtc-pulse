# Xtrata Builder Grant Budget Rationale ($35,000)

## Purpose of this document
This document explains exactly what the $35,000 Builder grant request funds,
why the split is structured this way, and what outcomes it is expected to
deliver.

## One-line summary
The budget is designed to de-risk core infrastructure first (audit), then
convert that reliability into adoption (docs/tooling + integrations), while
keeping enough team runway to execute and report measurable outcomes.

---

## Why request $35,000

## What this amount needs to achieve
1. Security confidence for ecosystem users and integrators.
2. Delivery runway to ship contract fixes, releases, and QA.
3. Developer adoption assets that reduce integration friction.
4. Early integration support with marketplaces and creator tools.
5. Public reporting so impact is visible and verifiable.

## Why not lower
A materially smaller budget would force tradeoffs between audit quality,
delivery capacity, and adoption work. That would increase launch risk and slow
ecosystem impact.

## Why not higher
The current request is intentionally scoped to a focused 4-6 month execution
window with clear deliverables, not an open-ended operating budget.

---

## Budget split and rationale

## 1) $12,000 - Third-party Clarity audit, remediation, verification (34%)
What this funds:
- External audit of core contract scope.
- Internal remediation work based on findings.
- Follow-up verification pass.

Why this amount:
- Audit is the highest-risk dependency and must be funded first.
- This allocation supports a focused but credible third-party review cycle
  without consuming most of the grant.

What success looks like:
- Audit completed.
- Findings addressed and documented.
- Verification completed on remediated contracts.

## 2) $11,000 - Core team runway: protocol engineering, QA, release management (31%)
What this funds:
- Engineering time for fixes, hardening, and contract/app integration updates.
- QA cycles and release coordination.
- Ongoing protocol maintenance during the grant window.

Why this amount:
- Audit findings and partner requests only create impact if a team can execute.
- This is targeted runway for shipping work, not full-salary replacement.

What success looks like:
- Predictable release cadence.
- Resolved defects and integrated audit remediations.
- Stable contract and app operations.

## 3) $7,000 - Developer adoption layer: docs, SDK/helpers, integration examples (20%)
What this funds:
- Integrator-facing documentation and quickstart improvements.
- Helper utilities/SDK components for common read and mint flows.
- Example implementations and reference patterns.

Why this amount:
- Infrastructure adoption depends on developer usability.
- This budget turns protocol capability into practical implementation value.

What success looks like:
- Improved developer onboarding.
- Faster integration time for third parties.
- Fewer repeated support questions from builders.

## 4) $3,000 - Ecosystem integration support (9%)
What this funds:
- Hands-on support for early marketplace and creator-tool integrations.
- Testing and coordination for partner go-lives.

Why this amount:
- Small but dedicated partner support budget helps convert inbound interest
  into live integrations.
- Most core engineering is already covered by runway.

What success looks like:
- New live integration(s) or partner pilots.
- Documented integration paths and playbooks.

## 5) $2,000 - Public analytics, reporting dashboards, reliability monitoring (6%)
What this funds:
- Baseline public reporting setup.
- Reliability and operations visibility.
- Grant reporting outputs.

Why this amount:
- Sufficient to prove outcomes without over-investing in internal ops tools.

What success looks like:
- Regularly updated metrics snapshots.
- Clear evidence of shipping and usage progression.

---

## What this budget intentionally does not fund
- Token emissions.
- Liquidity mining programs.
- Paid engagement or artificial growth tactics.

Reason:
Xtrata is positioned as infrastructure; budget use is focused on security,
delivery, adoption, and measurable ecosystem utility.

---

## 4-6 month execution model tied to this budget

## Phase 1 (Weeks 1-6): De-risk
- Contract audit kickoff and completion.
- Remediation planning and execution.

## Phase 2 (Weeks 7-12): Productize
- QA and release cycles for remediated contracts/workflows.
- Documentation and helper tooling improvements.

## Phase 3 (Weeks 13-24): Adopt and validate
- Partner integration support.
- Public reporting and reliability monitoring.
- Final outcomes summary.

---

## Expected outcomes from this split
1. Audited and hardened core contracts.
2. Stronger developer onboarding and integration throughput.
3. Live ecosystem integrations with lower support overhead.
4. Better transparency through reporting and operational metrics.

## Why this is a defensible Builder-track budget
- It prioritizes risk reduction before growth claims.
- It funds tangible deliverables instead of broad operating overhead.
- It aligns spending with infrastructure outcomes that reviewers can verify.
