# Concept

## Concise explanation

Self-Assembling / Remixable Films model a film as an editable graph of scenes, cuts, effects, sound layers, and narrative branches. Instead of treating the final export as the only canonical object, the ledger preserves how the film was assembled and how alternate cuts descend from shared source material.

## Problem being solved

Film production creates layered collaboration, but final distribution usually flattens everything into one render and one credit roll. Alternate cuts, restored editions, localized versions, and remix culture all lose structural continuity.

## Why ledger-native architecture helps

- timeline objects preserve scene-level and cut-level provenance
- alternate editions can reference the same source material without full duplication
- rights and payment logic can map to scenes, contributors, or distribution modes
- collectors and audiences can inspect how one cut differs from another

## Future expansion ideas

- audience-personalized cuts generated from licensed scene graphs
- collectible director's branch editions
- restoration projects that preserve conservation lineage alongside artistic lineage
