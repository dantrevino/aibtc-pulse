# Concept

## Concise explanation

Physical Objects with Life Histories treat a real-world item as a graph of lifecycle events. Manufacture, part replacement, certification, shipping, ownership, restoration, exhibition, and cultural use all become linked records instead of isolated paperwork.

## Problem being solved

Most provenance systems for physical goods stop at ownership or sale. They do not capture repair, restoration, component changes, or meaningful social history, which leaves value, authenticity, and care records fragmented.

## Why ledger-native architecture helps

- the object remains linked to its full lifecycle, not just title transfers
- parts and maintenance events can preserve authenticity and restoration context
- cultural usage history can contribute meaning and value beyond manufacture date
- insurers, collectors, and institutions can inspect the same source graph

## Future expansion ideas

- maintenance-aware insurance products
- museum-grade provenance graphs for artifacts and instruments
- circular-economy systems that reward repair and reuse
