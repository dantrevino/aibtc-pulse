# Concept

## Concise explanation

Skill Graphs Instead of CVs represent a person through the graph of artifacts they helped create, the teams they worked with, the reviews they received, and the learning paths they completed. The emphasis shifts from static narrative claims to verifiable contribution edges.

## Problem being solved

Resumes compress complex work into summaries that are easy to inflate and hard to verify. They rarely capture collaborative context, role changes over time, or the actual artifacts that demonstrate skill.

## Why ledger-native architecture helps

- artifacts and contribution edges can be verified directly
- collaboration becomes part of the identity model, not just a footnote
- reputation can follow real work lineage instead of isolated attestations
- portable graphs can outlive any one hiring platform or institution

## Future expansion ideas

- skill passports for guilds, DAOs, and creator collectives
- credential graphs combining formal education with open work history
- talent discovery tools that traverse projects rather than keywords only
