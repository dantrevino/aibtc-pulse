# Concept

## Concise explanation

Creative DNA / Remix Genome models a creative work as a graph of reusable components plus the sequencing rules that combine them. A song, animation, poster, or executable artwork becomes a lineage record of how existing parts were selected, transformed, and recomposed.

## Problem being solved

Current creative tooling makes lineage hard to inspect once work is exported. Attribution is manual, remix rights are vague, and revenue rarely flows back through the actual dependency chain.

## Why ledger-native architecture helps

- immutable references make provenance inspectable
- graph edges preserve who contributed what and when
- recursive composition allows works to become building blocks for later works
- programmable payout policies can follow dependency edges automatically

## Future expansion ideas

- cross-media remix markets spanning audio, visual layers, and code
- collector interfaces that show a work's full ancestry and descendants
- license templates that define remixability at the component level
